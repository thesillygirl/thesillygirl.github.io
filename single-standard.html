Top 6 things you must do as a fresher in college

  • By Chehak Choudhary

Being a fresher in Delhi University is like travelling to a new country, you're totally clueless about what to do and yet fascinated by it also. Every fresher needs to know the way to channelize the new found enthusiasm and learn about the things that will make our college life experience more memorable. When I started my college life as a fresher, I wanted to explore some must do things in college. So through this article I’ll share my learnings with you.

1) College orientation: College orientation is basically a welcoming ceremony organized by the college to familiarize the students with the college. You must attend the college orientation as it will help you get to know all the insights about your college ranging from the placements to all the college alumni.

Normally on a college orientation the students are toured around the college by the professors but due to Covid-19, we all mostly got to see our college through the virtual tour only this time. So I would suggest you to not miss your college orientation and get as many glimpses and insights about your college on that day.

2) Skipping college classes: Every student enjoys bunking or skipping the boring college classes for doing anything fun with your friends or just chilling for some time. In school, I was a very diligent student so I didn’t get the taste of doing little mischiefs like bunking classes but as I became a college student, I tried skipping classes.

I must tell you that it is really fun and the professors also don’t bound you to attend the lectures like teachers did in school which gives us a whole new freedom but this fun experience has also been spoiled by the pandemic as all of us are having online classes and there is no fun in skipping the classes as we can simply leave the class with a click or not even join them.

Due to this new normal we the students have found a new alternative to skipping classes which is sleeping through our classes as our audio and videos are off during our classes but I really wish that everything gets normal like previous times and we get to experience the real essence of our college life which is skipping our college classes.

3) College societies: College societies are primarily the platforms where you can work you’re your fellow college students in different domains like poetry, dance, environment, social work and many more by becoming a member of these societies. Everyone has a different opinion regarding college societies, some people think that they don't add much value to our growth while some think that they help us in developing our personalities.

I can tell you one thing surely that if you pursue something with your heart and do your best in it, then it surely leaves a positive impact on your personality. We should definitely try and become a part of a college society because it really helps in enhancing your social skills, knowledge and exposure. I have personally learned a lot while being a part of college societies and they have also increased my number of connections as I worked on different projects.

If you ever ask anybody on how many societies we should join ideally then everybody would mostly tell you that you should join as many as possible but I would suggest you to join the societies according to your capability of handling multiple things as it gets difficult to manage sometimes with your studies.

4) Internships: Internships can be understood as a period of work experience with different firms or organizations. They are of two types mainly - Paid and Unpaid internships. As a fresher, you must do an internship because it helps us in learning professional skills, getting real world experience and adds to your character growth.

In my first year, I did many internships and believe me they were worth my time as it broadened my perspectives on things and helped me make some memories with some new people. While pursuing an internship I not only enhanced my professional and social skills but also improved my time management and soft skills.

An internship can be of any profile according to your interests and you can do what you love by providing it as a service you offer. Many companies that come to colleges expect the students to have some work experience and be familiar with professional skills required in the company which they judge by the internships pursued by the student, so we can say that internships also play an important role in college placements.

5) Competitions and workshops: Competitions and workshops are one of the ways that we can enjoy ourselves and learn something new apart from our academics. In this pandemic era, these competitions and workshops have become the most popular way to display our talents and socialize with others. Participating in these activities can help us utilize our time efficiently and step out of our comfort zones by challenging ourselves to try something new.

College societies hold many workshops, webinars, quizzes and competitions and even offer a bunch of exciting rewards and certificates. You can also add the positions and awards achieved in competitions and workshops to your CV to show that you are good in extra-curricular activities also.

6) Pursuing add-on courses: Everyone studies the courses taken by them as their major but have you thought that what makes us different from others or helps us stand out in the crowd in college placements? Well, the answer is add-on courses. They are basically some courses a student can pursue simultaneously with their studies based on their interests. It can include language courses or professional courses.

In my case, I have been pursuing French language course. I have always been fascinated by the beauty of this soft and yet wonderful language so I decided to pursue this language. From my personal experience, I can tell you that sometimes it can get difficult to manage with your studies and you can get frustrated also but always remember why you started.

To pursue any course the most important traits you must possess are passion, determination and hardwork. These qualities have helped me in my journey of learning a new language and staying motivated throughout this journey. So I’ll suggest you to definitely pursue a course apart from your studies that intrigues you.
